# Voice Constant, Tone Flexes

The core mental model behind every brand-voice skill. **Voice** is who the brand is — it never
changes. **Tone** is how that voice adapts to a situation — it flexes constantly. Get this
distinction right and content stays recognizably the brand across every channel while still
fitting the moment.

## Voice vs. tone

| | Voice | Tone |
|-|-|-|
| What it is | The brand's fixed personality | Situational adjustment of that personality |
| Changes? | Never | Every context |
| Example | "We are direct and proof-driven" | Warmer in a support reply, sharper on X |
| Captured as | We Are / We Are Not | Tone-by-context matrix |

A brand that is "direct and proof-driven" is direct and proof-driven in a launch tweet, a
condolence-adjacent apology, and a sales proposal. What flexes is the *register* — formality,
energy, technical depth — not the identity.

## We Are / We Are Not (the voice constants)
Voice is defined as paired attributes and boundaries. Each "We Are" trait names what the brand
IS; each "We Are Not" names the failure mode right next to it that the brand must never slip into.

| We Are | We Are Not |
|-|-|
| Direct | Blunt / cold |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Proof-driven | Hype-driven |
| Plain-spoken | Dumbed-down |

The boundary column matters as much as the attribute: "confident but not arrogant" tells a
writer exactly where the edge is. Crossing a "We Are Not" boundary is the most serious voice
failure — heavier than merely under-expressing a "We Are" trait.

## The three tone dimensions
Tone flexes along three independent dials. Every context in the matrix is a setting of these three.

### Formality
| Level | Reads like |
|-|-|
| Low | Contractions, casual, first-name energy, emoji OK |
| Medium | Professional but human; contractions fine; no slang |
| High | Precise, structured, no contractions, no slang |

### Energy
| Level | Reads like |
|-|-|
| Low | Calm, measured, matter-of-fact |
| Medium | Engaged, warm, forward-moving |
| High | Punchy, urgent, exclamation-adjacent (without hype) |

### Technical depth
| Level | Reads like |
|-|-|
| Low | Plain language, no jargon, outcome-focused |
| Medium | Some domain terms, explained in context |
| High | Full domain vocabulary, assumes an expert reader |

## The tone-by-context matrix
Map each context the brand writes in to a setting of the three dials. Voice stays constant down
every column; only the dials move.

| Context | Formality | Energy | Technical depth |
|-|-|-|-|
| LinkedIn post | Medium | High | Low |
| X / short-form | Low | High | Low |
| Cold email | Medium | Medium | Low |
| Support reply | Low | Medium | Low-Medium |
| Proposal / deck | High | Medium | High |
| Blog / long-form | Medium | Medium | High |

## Applying voice + tone together
1. **Lock voice first.** Load the We Are / We Are Not attributes — these are non-negotiable.
2. **Pick the context row.** Match the request to a matrix row; set the three dials.
3. **Write with both.** Every sentence expresses the voice constants; the register follows the dials.
4. **Check boundaries.** Nothing crosses a "We Are Not." Terminology follows must-use/never.
5. **Validate.** Voice attributes present + tone matches the row + no boundary crossed = on-brand.

Example: a "direct, proof-driven" brand announcing a Series A on LinkedIn → voice constant
(direct, proof-driven), tone dialed to formality Medium / energy High / depth Low → "We closed
$12M to put platform engineering on autopilot. Here's the number that convinced our lead investor:
…" — high energy, but the proof leads and there's zero hype. Voice held; tone flexed.

## Common enforcement mistakes
- **Flexing voice instead of tone.** Making the brand "more casual" by dropping a core attribute
  (e.g. abandoning proof-driven for a fun tweet). Wrong — keep the attribute, lower the formality dial.
- **Ignoring the boundary column.** Writing "confident" copy that reads arrogant. The "We Are Not"
  is the guardrail; check it explicitly.
- **One tone everywhere.** Using the LinkedIn register in a support reply or a proposal. Match the row.
- **Hype smuggled in as energy.** High energy ≠ hype. Raise urgency with verbs and specifics,
  not with "game-changing" / "thrilled" / "revolutionary".
- **Terminology drift under tone pressure.** Casual context is not license to break must-use/never terms.
